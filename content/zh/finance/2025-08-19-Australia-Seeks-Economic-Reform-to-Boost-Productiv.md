Australian Treasurer Jim Chalmers is hosting a gathering of politicians, experts and business leaders in a bid to discuss productivity. It comes at a time where the country's performance is lagging the most in the developed world. Speaking with Bloomberg: The Asia Trade, e61 Institute CEO and Former Australian Productivity Commission Chair Michael Brennan says a more dynamic labor force is needed, to achieve the nation's goals.
