The thin and light iPhone Air does not have a physical SIM slot, and is limited to eSIM-only operation. That's something of a problem, because in China, carriers do not support eSIM even though it's widely used around the world. Until now, iPhones sold in China have always had a physical SIM slot, and that's still the case for the iPhone 17, 17 Pro, and 17 Pro Max. There wasn't space for a SIM tray in the &zwnj;iPhone&zwnj; Air, but China won't have to go without. Apple was able to work with China Unicom to bring the &zwnj;iPhone&zwnj; Air with eSIM to the Chinese market. On its website in China, Apple says that customers will need an eSIM-supported carrier and mobile plan to use the &zwnj;iPhone&zwnj; Air. The company also clarifies that not all carriers support eSIM, and that customers who want to use the &zwnj;iPhone&zwnj; Air will need to sign up for a plan with China Unicom.
