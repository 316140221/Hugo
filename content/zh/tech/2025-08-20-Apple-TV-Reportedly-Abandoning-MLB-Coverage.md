Apple is apparently set to exit its partnership with Major League Baseball and discontinue its "Friday Night Baseball" package on Apple TV+. Apple first entered Major League Baseball in March 2022 with a seven-year agreement valued at roughly &#36;85 million per year, which granted it exclusive global streaming rights to two Friday night games each week on &zwnj;Apple TV&zwnj;+. The deal was originally scheduled to run through 2029, but Yahoo Sports' Kendall Baker now says that Apple has exercised an early opt-out provision, allowing the company to end its involvement before the contract's planned expiration. Apple's exit from baseball arrives less than three full seasons into its seven-year arrangement, marking a rare early termination of a major streaming sports contract. Baker described the new arrangement as "close to being done." The shift would see Apple leave Major League Baseball coverage entirely.
