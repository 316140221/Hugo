ASUS recently came out with a new 32-inch 6K ProArt Display that's priced at &#36;1,399, so we thought we'd check it out to see how it compares with the Studio Display and the Pro Display XDR from Apple. Subscribe to the MacRumors YouTube channel for more videos. At 32 inches, the ProArt 6K is larger than the 27-inch Studio Display, but it is on par in size with the Pro Display XDR. ASUS doesn't have the same design aesthetic as Apple, so the ProArt 6K's design isn't impressive. There's a square-shaped base, an arm that attaches to the display, and thin bezels at the top and the sides. There's a thicker bottom bezel that houses some quick access control buttons. ASUS' display has the same 218 pixels per inch as the Pro Display XDR, and text looks crisp. Colors are accurate out of the box and can be further tweaked in the Settings menu with different profiles.
